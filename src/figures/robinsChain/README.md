# Robins chain

Parameters:
- default 8 beats
- a FoilRelationship

Displays like "[robins chain] to your [neighbor]"

Final keyframe:

How the dancers move: 
- use the foil relationship to make (lark, robin) pairs; call this lark the robin's "receiver".
- each robin should be on the other side of the set from her lark (x<0, x>0)
- when a robin looks at her lark on the other side of the set, there is another lark on her left; call this her "sender"
- assert that, if Alice is being sent by Bob to Charlie, then some robin is being sent by Charlie to Bob; this is the opposite (with some offset) of the original robin
- The two robins each walk towards their center of mass until they're 1.3m apart (approx 1/8 of the time)
- the two robins pull by right hands. while they do, each sender lark steps sideways into his sendee's original spot, and faces the robin he's receiving. (approx 2/8 of the time)
- each robin walks to her reciever's original spot, while her receiver turns so that she's on his right. (approx 1/8 of the time)
- the two join left hands and join right hands, and rotate 180 degrees ccw around their shared center of mass; then drop right hands. (approx 1/2 of the time)

The final keyframe should be the Larks in the same place as they originally were, with their original facing, while the robins have traded places and each has adopted her receiver's facing.
